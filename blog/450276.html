The Best Travel Apps 2024

Home Telecom
 

Summer is around the corner, and there’s no time like the present to start booking your vacations and getaways. If you’re looking for a convenient way to plan your next trip, download these best travel apps 2024, which will help streamline your vacation planning experience!

Best Travel Apps for Planning and Organization

  • Roadtrippers — If you love finding interesting destinations and sites to stop at while on vacation, this app is for you! Explore maps of the U.S. and Canada and find unique museums, campsites, roadside attractions and more. To help you keep track of sites, you can add them to your trip itinerary, and the app will automatically provide the best route for you to take to get there. The app can only access certain features via paid membership tiers depending on how many stops and trips you use the app for.
  • Hopper — You’ll want to download this app before buying airline tickets. One of the best travel apps 2024, Hopper tracks flight prices and provides detailed advice on when the best time to purchase tickets is by providing you with price drop notifications. Hopper goes further than other apps and will give the date the airline price will increase.
  • TripCase — This free app will organize your trip and help you make a custom chronological itinerary. Imagine having all your accommodations, flights, restaurant reservations and even rental cars in one convenient location. (iOS, Android)

Best Travel Apps for Booking

  • Airbnb — The ultimate vacation rental site. (iOS, Android)
  • Booking.com — Deals on flights, hotels, car rentals and cruises.
  • Expedia — Flights, cars, hotels and vacation packages. (iOS, Android)
  • Hotels.com — This app specializes in hotels and car rentals. (iOS, Android)
  • Hotel Tonight — As the name implies, this app is for last-minute hotel deals.
  • Hotwire — Hotels, cars, flights and vacation packages.
  • Kayak — Compare prices on flights, hotels, cars and vacation packages.
  • Orbitz — Hotels, flights, cars, vacation packages, things to do and cruises.
  • Priceline — Score deals on hotels, flights, cars and cruises.
  • Vrbo — Similar to Airbnb, which offers vacation rentals.
  • Culture Trip — While this site was originally designed to read about travel destinations, it now primarily offers package tours.
  • Roomer Travel — If you’ve ever pre-paid for a hotel room and then had to cancel, you know the pain of losing that money. Whether you’re posting a pre-paid hotel room or looking to score a deal, this website lets you explore hotels that people are trying to sell pre-paid rooms by date and location. (iOS, Android)
  • Viator — If you prefer to book tours, check out Viator. Whether looking for a daily excursion or a weeklong tour, you can quickly search for tours that meet your criteria.
  • Wanderu — This is an excellent option for booking buses and trains.

Best Travel Apps for Camping

  • The Dyrt — Search, find and save campsites.
  • Glamping Hub — The ultimate glamping resource site. (iOS, Android)
  • Harvest Hosts — Overnight camping at over 5,000 participating farms, wineries, breweries and more.
  • Hipcamp — Reserve private RV sites and campsites.
  • Tentrr — A great camping and glamping site. (iOS, Android)
  • iOverlander — This app is designed for overlanders and is based on participants’ information and reviews.

Best Travel Apps for Navigating

  • AllTrails — This fantastic outdoor app lists all hiking and walking trails, including maps, pictures and even directions to nearly hidden trailheads. Download the trail maps offline for more remote areas if you sign up for a paid version.
  • Convertr+ — Quickly convert dollars into other currencies while on vacation.
  • GasBuddy — Track gas prices while on vacation and see who has the cheapest gas.
  • Google Maps — Stay connected with GPS navigation, get traffic alerts, explore transit information and even get walking directions. (iOS, Android)
  • iExit — This navigation app focuses on powerful simplicity and lets you search for businesses and amenities.
  • Waze — Waze provides real-time traffic alerts so you can quickly be rerouted and arrive at your destination.
  • Uber — If you don’t want to rent a car while on vacation, opt for an Uber and download the app beforehand.
